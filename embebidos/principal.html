About me

 

I am a researcher with the AISII at the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana-Azcapotzalco (UAM-A) in Mexico City. My research areas are wireless networks and information systems. I received the B.Sc. in Computer from the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana of Mexico in 2008. Later, I received a M. Sc. in Science and Information Technologies from Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana of Mexico in 2011. Finally, I received my PhD in Science and Information Technologies from Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana of Mexico in 2016. I'm very thankful with my advisor PhD. Víctor Manuel Ramos Ramos and the WINETSYS for supporting me during my stay at UAMI.


Wireless Networking Systems Research Group

 

Led by Dr. Victor Ramos, the Wireless Network Systems (WiNetSys) is a graduate research group intended to develop, implement, and analyze the performance of medium access mechanisms for a wide span of wireless systems based on the IEEE 802.11, such as heterogeneous radio access technologies, vehicular and mesh networks, as well as Radio Frequency IDentification systems (RFID) under the EPCglobal specifications.
